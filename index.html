Au pays des aveugles, les borgnes sont rois In the land of the blind, the one-eyed man is king

This triptych illustrates the proverb in three parts, each poster taking the form of a playing card. The use of simple colors and austere shapes, inspired by propaganda posters, highlights the severity and symbolic weight of the message.

Collaboration with two first-year graphic design students, Fanny Bachelet and Enzo Epiard

/!\ SOS /!\ Objets Fantômes /!\ SOS /!\ Ghostly Objects

A photographic exploration about perfume bottles. Through reframing, close-ups and bitmap effects, their contours dissolve into spectral silhouettes. Printed in cyanotype, these objects become enigmatic, ghosts of past possessions.

Le carnet d'été The summer sketchbook

This sketchbook recounts my summer 2021 while experimenting with the style of Carl Johanson in his fanzine I forgot - A collection of forgetfulness. Clear lines, flat-coloured areas, synthetic shapes, contrast and narration guide the visual storytelling.

Voyage Graphique Graphic Travel

Drawings from the 2021 sketchbook were reused to create postcards mentioning my Graphic travel and creative research. Superimposed yellow, cyan, and magenta layers create depth and rhythm.

Ponctuellement Punctually

Created during the integration workshop Ponctually, this project focuses on the asterisk as a graphic sign. Through extraction, repetition, cutting, and mise en abyme, the symbol becomes a rhythmic element. Risography emphasizes these shifts with vibrant textures.

Collaboration with a first-year graphic design student Éléonore W.

Nos invisibles Our unseen ones

Four photographic booklets and a written booklet built around the expression Wipe the slate clean. The series focuses on those who actually use the sponge, whose essential yet invisible labor often goes unrecognized. Photographs and texts draw on field testimonies, the film Debout les Femmes by François Ruffin and Stromae's song Santé.

L'affiche partout, ma place nulle part Posters everywhere, my place nowhere

This thesis questions the citizen's position in public spaces saturated by visual signs, particularly advertising. Posters can create spaces for reflection, representation, visibility, creativity, imagination, and exchange, leading to a reappropriation of public spaces as places for living and meeting.

Bienvenue Chez Vous Welcome home

Following the teasing principle, this project unfolds across three timeframes. Each poster announces a round-table discussion, led by contributors animating debates and reflections on specific themes such as the importance of critical thinking, imagination, and reflection in public spaces and displays. In the meantime, stickers and leaflets encourage engagement with the campaign's visual language. The project aims to question the role of images in public spaces and to reclaim streets and squares as places of encounter.

Do Research, Make Work, and Let's Talk About It

Based on Daniel van der Velden essay Research & Destroy: Graphic Design as Investigation (2006), which questions the role of designers in society. We selected short key excerpts and reformatted them as an email hypothetically addressed to other designers. The text was re-encoded using typographic substitutions and became partly encrypted and dynamic.

Broken Transitions, Embracing Imperfection

Perfection is a myth and reality is messy. Broken Transitions is a trilogy of editorial artifacts exploring the creative potential of error through three categories: unintended errors (technical, human, material), reclaimed errors (discovered and preserved), and provoked errors (deliberate misuse or sabotage). Each volume develops a distinct graphic identity shaped by flaws, misalignments, overlaps, and typographic manipulation. The project questions the pursuit of perfection in design, reframing errors as creative opportunities.
